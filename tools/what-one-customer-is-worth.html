Example: You make R800 a sale. They buy 3 times a year, for 4 years. R800 × 3 × 4 = R9,600. That's what one customer is really worth.
What this means. Most owners only think about the first sale — and undersell everything after it. Now compare this to what a customer costs you to win in the first place. A good rule: a customer should be worth at least 3× more than they cost.
